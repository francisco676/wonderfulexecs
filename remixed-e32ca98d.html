State of Authority + Pilot
Prepared for Ilan and the Wonderful brand team

Wonderful's GMs are the brand. The product is global. The trust is local.

Wonderful's whole thesis is culturally fluent agents in markets where most vendors don't show up. The GMs are the human face of that. They're the ones telco execs in Greece and bank CFOs in Italy actually trust to deliver. Which means their personal brands aren't a nice-to-have. They're a defensible moat. The question is which ones to invest in first, and how to do it without producing brown-chair posts that hurt rather than help.

Where you're at

Some GMs are silent. Others post a lot, but about the wrong things.

You said it on the call: there are GMs at Wonderful posting frequently and getting 600 likes on takes that have nothing to do with what Wonderful actually does. Vanity engagement on off-brand content. The instinct is right to push back on it. Engagement that doesn't reach your ICP isn't just useless. It can dilute the brand.

"It often borders a little bit on ridiculous slash irrelevant. Despite the vanity metrics of impressions and likes, it's not something that's talking to your ICP."

Ilan, on the call

On the other side, GMs like Omer have everything that makes a great brand voice (operator background, regional credibility, the kind of story that lands with enterprise buyers in their market) but aren't posting regularly. The opportunity is in the gap between those two groups. Not pushing the wrong people to do more, not silencing the wrong people, but figuring out who's sitting on the biggest unrealized leverage and unlocking exactly that.

Our principle

Audience relevance over engagement vanity.

Most ghostwriters optimize for likes. We optimize for the right eyes. Three hundred people from your ICP reading a thoughtful take on multilingual agent deployment in regulated industries beats six hundred unrelated likes on a brown-chair post every time. That's the principle this entire engagement is built on.

The metric we actually care about
An audience of 1,000 telco execs in Italy is worth more than 100,000 random likes.
A real audience is the people who buy what you sell, the people who fund what you build, and the people you want to hire. We measure what we do against that bar, not against how the post performed in absolute numbers.
