Our principle

Audience relevance over engagement vanity.

Most ghostwriters optimize for likes. We optimize for the right eyes. Three hundred people from your ICP reading a thoughtful take on multilingual agent deployment in regulated industries beats six hundred unrelated likes on a brown-chair post every time. That's the principle this entire engagement is built on.

The metric we actually care about
An audience of 1,000 telco execs in Italy is worth more than 100,000 random likes.
A real audience is the people who buy what you sell, the people who fund what you build, and the people you want to hire. We measure what we do against that bar, not against how the post performed in absolute numbers.
Where the leverage is

The GMs are your biggest lever. Time is the cost of waiting.

You said it on the 20 minute call: "Wonderful's GMs are the brand. The product is global. The trust is local." Everything that makes Wonderful different in a buying conversation runs through the GM in the room. Not the product page. Not the press release. The named operator standing in the market the buyer cares about.

Why the GM is the lever, structurally.

  • They are the only differentiator that compounds. A product feature ships once. A press release runs once. A GM posting in the buyer's language for 9 months builds a body of work the buyer can search, validate, and route around their own gatekeepers.
  • They are the only differentiator competitors can't acquire. Software gets cloned, geographic presence gets bought, partnerships get matched. A real person with credibility in one market, posting in their voice, is the one moat that doesn't transfer with a check.
  • They are the only differentiator that scales without diluting. A central marketing voice gets thinner with every market it enters. 25 GMs each owning their market is the opposite. Wonderful gets sharper in every conversation it shows up in.

And every dimension competitors can buy, they're buying. Fast.

The reason time matters isn't pressure from us. It's that every other layer of Wonderful's positioning is consolidating around you while you wait.
